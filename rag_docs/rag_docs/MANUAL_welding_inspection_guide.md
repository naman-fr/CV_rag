# Welding Inspection Guide & Rework Manual

## 1. Defect Identification & Verification

### A. Cracks (Hot & Cold Cracks)
- **Visual Description**: Linear ruptures visible on the weld bead or heat-affected zone (HAZ). Can be longitudinal or transverse.
- **Severity**: **Critical**. No cracks are allowed under any circumstances.
- **Rework**: Grinding out the weld bead completely and re-welding according to WPS-012.

### B. Undercut
- **Visual Description**: A groove melted into the base metal adjacent to the toe of the weld and left unfilled by weld metal.
- **Severity**:
  - Undercut depth > 1.0mm is a **Major Defect** (Requires fill-welding).
  - Undercut depth <= 1.0mm is a **Minor Defect** (Acceptable if length is < 50mm).

### C. Porosity (Gas Cavities)
- **Visual Description**: Spherical or elongated voids caused by entrapped gas bubbles. Appears as small circular holes on the weld face.
- **Severity**:
  - Cluster porosity (> 3 pores in a 10mm area) is a **Major Defect**.
  - Isolated pores < 1mm are **Minor Defects**.

## 2. Visual Inspection Checklist
Inspectors must verify:
- Complete penetration of the joint.
- Absence of surface slag.
- Weld bead profile uniformity.
