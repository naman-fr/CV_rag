# Taxonomy of Casting Surface Anomalies

This document catalogs casting anomalies, their visual markers, and quality control tolerance thresholds.

## 1. Blowholes and Pinholes
- **Description**: Smooth, rounded cavities inside the casting or on its surface caused by entrapped mold gases.
- **Visual Marker**: Circular depressions resembling craters.
- **QC Threshold**: 
  - Diameter > 3mm: **Major Defect** (Rework via welding/plugging).
  - Diameter <= 3mm: **Minor Defect** (Acceptable, limit 2 per surface).

## 2. Cold Shuts
- **Description**: An interface where two streams of liquid metal meet but fail to fuse cleanly due to low temperature or premature freezing.
- **Visual Marker**: A distinct crack-like line with rounded edges on the casting surface.
- **QC Threshold**: **Critical Defect** (Causes mechanical shear failure. Part scrap).

## 3. Shrinkage Cavities
- **Description**: Voids formed during metal solidification due to volume shrinkage.
- **Visual Marker**: Irregular, jagged-edged cavernous depressions, often in the thicker sections of the casting.
- **QC Threshold**: **Critical Defect** if depth exceeds 5% of section thickness.
