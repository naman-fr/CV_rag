## Document Reference: GF-SLA-2025-v3.1
## Effective Date: 01 January 2025 | Review Date: 31 December 2025

---

## 1. PARTIES

This Service Level Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into between **GlobalFreight Logistics Pvt. Ltd.** ("Carrier") and any registered shipper operating under a valid GlobalFreight account ("Shipper").

---

## 2. SERVICE TIERS

### 2.1 Platinum Express
- **Delivery Commitment:** Door-to-door in 2–3 business days (domestic); 5–7 business days (international)
- **Tracking Updates:** Real-time, every 2 hours
- **Delay Tolerance:** Maximum 4 hours beyond committed delivery window
- **Compensation:** 15% refund of freight charges for each 24-hour delay block beyond tolerance
- **Priority Handling:** Dedicated loading bay; first-loaded last-offloaded
- **Customer Notification:** Mandatory proactive notification if delay > 2 hours

### 2.2 Gold Standard
- **Delivery Commitment:** Door-to-door in 3–5 business days (domestic); 7–10 business days (international)
- **Tracking Updates:** Every 6 hours
- **Delay Tolerance:** Maximum 12 hours beyond committed delivery window
- **Compensation:** 10% refund of freight charges for each 24-hour delay block beyond tolerance
- **Customer Notification:** Mandatory proactive notification if delay > 6 hours

### 2.3 Standard Economy
- **Delivery Commitment:** Door-to-door in 5–7 business days (domestic); 10–14 business days (international)
- **Tracking Updates:** Once per day (18:00 local origin timezone)
- **Delay Tolerance:** Maximum 48 hours beyond committed delivery window
- **Compensation:** 5% refund of freight charges for each 48-hour delay block beyond tolerance
- **Customer Notification:** Notification only if delay > 24 hours

## 4. CUSTOMS CLEARANCE

### 4.1 Standard Clearance Times
- **India Customs (Export):** 4–8 hours post-submission of complete documentation
- **EU Customs (Import):** 1–2 business days
- **UAE Customs:** 4–6 hours
- **Singapore Customs:** 2–4 hours (for pre-approved shippers: 1 hour)
- **US Customs:** 1–3 business days (may extend to 5 days for high-value or restricted goods)
- **Japan Customs:** 1–2 business days

### 4.2 Documentation Requirements (International)
The following documents must be submitted **minimum 4 hours before** scheduled departure:
1. Commercial Invoice (3 copies)
2. Packing List (itemized, including weights)
3. Bill of Lading / Air Waybill
4. Certificate of Origin (for preferential tariff treatment)
5. HS Code Declaration (8-digit, mandatory)
6. Shipper's Export Declaration (SED)
7. Insurance Certificate (for shipments > USD 10,000)

**Missing HS Code:** Shipment will be held until correct HS code is provided. SLA clock pauses during hold.

### 4.3 Pre-clearance Programme
Shippers with Platinum tier and minimum 12 months account history may apply for Pre-Clearance Approval. Benefits:
- Customs clearance reduced to 2 hours (India), 4 hours (EU)
- Dedicated customs liaison officer
- Digital documentation accepted

## 5. FORCE MAJEURE & EXCLUSIONS

SLA commitments are suspended (SLA clock paused) in the following events:
- Natural disasters (floods, earthquakes, cyclones) declared by government authority
- Port strikes or labour action — carrier is not liable; shipper may request route diversion at additional cost
- Pandemic-related border closures
- Cyber incidents affecting carrier IT systems (maximum 48-hour SLA pause)
- War, conflict, or government embargo on specific routes

**Notification obligation:** Carrier must notify shipper within 2 hours of a Force Majeure event affecting their shipment.

---

## 6. CLAIMS PROCESS

- **Filing window:** Claims must be filed within 14 calendar days of original committed delivery date
- **Documentation required:** Proof of shipment, commercial invoice, delivery confirmation, damage photos if applicable
- **Resolution time:** 10 business days for standard claims; 5 business days for Platinum claims
- **Dispute escalation:** Unresolved claims escalate to Logistics Dispute Board within 30 days
